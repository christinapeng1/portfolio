    HowToStudyKorean.com

    Posted on March 13, 2025

    Overview

    How to Study Korean is a website with free lessons for people trying to learn Korean. I used it over the summer when I was trying to self-study Hangul.

    I'm so grateful to the instructor for making these lessons available online for free. As a passion project, I am going to redesign the website based on my experience using it as a student to improve its usability.

    Identifying the Problem

    Observations

    • There are limited color contrast
    • Poor efficiency, have to scroll past multiple layers to enter the lessons
    • Poor learnability, Unclear user journe; the structure of dividng into units / lessons is a good call like a textbook but it's not made clear in the initial navigation/home page
    • Good memoriability
    • Conceptual model

    Using WebAIM WAVE

    I ran the website into WebAIM Wave. WebAIM detected a lot of problems with color contrast

    • 5 linked images missing alt text
    • 56 very low contrast errors
    • 9 alerts concerning suspicious alt/link text, 6 redundant title/link text, 1 skipped heading level
    • 5 features
    • 37 structural elements
    • 13 ARIA

    I agree with some but not all of the findings. I think it does point out many low contrast errors, and the lack of alt text and accessibility features. I'm not sure about the suspicious alt/link text or what it means by redundant title/link text.
